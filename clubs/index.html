Social Life at St. Andrew's Church

As well as the opportunity for fellowship each Sunday at worship and at the informal refreshments after the 9.30 am service, we have an active and varied calendar of events, sometimes these are for fund-raising and sometimes merely for pleasure!

We hold occasional Sunday lunches and Parish Teas which are extremely popular.

Other events include Quiz Nights, Poetry and Puddings evenings, tea in the Vicarage Garden, barn dances, promises Auctions – almost anything that includes food and the chance to meet, chat and have fun!

Each year group bookings are made for theatre trips to Worthing and Chichester and between 40 and 100 folk participate.

Groups

GIN: (Girls in the Neighbourhood): We have two social events a year. In the summer, usually July, we meet in a restaurant for a meal. In December we have an 'in house' social evening with food to which partners are invited and for the second half of that evening we have some form of musical entertainment.

Our speakers are varied and include talks and demonstrations.

Contact: Wendy Hughes on 01903 243526

Pathway Group: Friendly and supportive Bible Study Groups for women, held on mid-week afternoons and evenings in private homes.

Contact: Sharon Miller on 01903 242327

Seedlings: Seedlings is open to all Mothers/Carers/Grandparents of babies and toddlers in the Ferring and Goring areas only.

We meet every Friday during school term time in the Church Centre for two hours 9.15 – 11.15 a.m., where the children engage in playing and interacting with each other. A planned and varied craft activity is provided and there is always singing with actions including the use of musical instruments.

The cost of each session is £1.50 per adult which includes coffee/tea/squash and biscuits served by our wonderful kitchen team.

Contact: Lynn Stevenson on 01903 241839
or Emma Seager on 01903 911682

St. Andrew's Table Tennis Group: The Thursday Table Tennis Group is a mixed group (both in terms of ability and gender!) and although quite competitive, we primarily meet for the exercise and the friendship.

If you’d like to see if it’s your 'cup of tea', either pop into the Church Centre on a Thursday between 7.30 and 9.30 in the evening, or ring Margaret Metcalf (see below).

We look forward to hearing from you!

Contact: Margaret Metcalf on 01903 244420

^ Back to top

Trefoil Guild: The Guild represents 'Guiding for Grown-ups' and is part of the Girl Guiding movement. Membership is open to anyone with an interest in guiding, but it does not matter whether you have been a previous member of either guiding or scouting. Our current membership is all female, but husbands and partners join us on many occasions.

We currently meet on the second Wednesday of the month at 2:30-4:00 the afternoon in the Church Centre.

Contact: Ruth Sims 01903 243212
